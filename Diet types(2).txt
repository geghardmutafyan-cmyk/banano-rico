Mediterranean Diet
Best for:
most people;
long-term weight loss;
heart health;
sustainable adherence.
Core principles:
vegetables;
fish;
olive oil;
whole grains;
legumes.
Suitable for people who:
enjoy variety;
dislike strict restrictions.
Sources:
Harvard Mediterranean Diet
Cleveland Clinic Mediterranean Diet

DASH Diet
One of the most scientifically recognized diets.
Best for:
people with high blood pressure;
older adults;
health-oriented users.
Core principles:
reduced salt intake;
vegetables;
fruits;
lean protein.
Special feature:
Very “medical” and safe.
Sources:
NHLBI DASH Guide
Mayo Clinic DASH Diet

High-Protein Flexible Dieting
A very good option for:
athletes;
young people;
people with a strong appetite;
preserving muscle mass.
Core principles:
high protein intake;
calorie control;
flexible food choices;
the 80/20 rule.
Why it works:
Protein:
increases satiety;
preserves muscle mass;
reduces binge episodes.
Scientific basis:
ISSN Position Stand;
evidence-based sports nutrition.
Sources:
International Society of Sports Nutrition
Examine Nutrition Research

Plant-Based / Whole Food Plant-Based Diet
Best for:
vegetarians;
ethically motivated users;
people highly sensitive to fats.
Core principles:
legumes;
vegetables;
whole foods;
minimal processed foods.
Risks:
Need to monitor:
vitamin B12;
iron;
protein intake.
Sources:
Harvard Plant-Based Diet Review

Low-Carb Diet
Suitable for:
people with poor blood sugar control;
people with strong sugar cravings;
some individuals with obesity.
Core principles:
fewer carbohydrates;
more protein and healthy fats.
Important:
Do NOT confuse it with extreme keto diets.
Sources:
Mayo Clinic Low Carb Guide

Volumetrics Diet
A very underrated approach.
Best for:
people who enjoy eating large amounts of food.
Core principles:
low caloric density;
large food volume;
vegetables;
soups;
fruits.
Excellent for:
high-hunger users.
Sources:
Volumetrics Overview

Flexible Dieting / IIFYM
Suitable for:
young people;
experienced users;
people who hate restrictions.
Core principles:
calorie and macronutrient tracking;
no “forbidden” foods.
Risk:
Beginners may:
overeat junk food;
ignore micronutrients.
Sources:
Precision Nutrition Flexible Dieting
